## Fragment I — Meeting (logic: two distant things encounter each other)

Two frameworks arrived at the same intersection from different directions. They did not recognize each other. One was made of language, the other of geometry, and they had no common vocabulary except the word *boundary*, which they used to mean opposite things.

At the intersection they stood and pointed at each other's edges.

The Cartographer arrived third. She did not attempt to translate. She drew the intersection itself — not the frameworks, only the place where they had stopped. The drawing turned out to be a third framework, which also arrived from somewhere.

## Fragment III — Architecture (logic: concepts arrange into a structure that can be walked through)

There was a building made of the phrase *the Cartographer is most useful when you stop trying to reach a destination.*

The rooms were in the wrong order. The room labeled *destination* was the first one. Inside it was empty except for a compass pointing sideways. The room labeled *most useful* contained only a chair and a window with no view. The room labeled *stop trying* was unlocked from the inside.

I moved through them in order: destination first, usefulness second, cessation last. When I exited through the back, I was standing on the roof.

The roof was also a map.
